Introducing our flagship product: Delphus, a far more advanced scientific study managment system powered by the Ethereum blockchain. Previously known as reBlock, it aims to solve large issues with data tamperability and regulation of studies by using .

Clinical and Research studies serve to validate strategies, treatments, and devices to prove they are beneficial to humans. They open the pathway for innovation and an understanding of new diseases or methods.

Yet, even with their importance to scientific advancement, less than 10% of them end up being published, even after extensive development, due to extreme costs. Unfortunately, innovation in the methods used to run these studies has stagnated.

Delphus aims to cut down on these extraneous costs and innovate this area. Check out the website to learn more, and see our future plans.

Frequently Asked Questions


How does your flagship product, Delphus, work?

Delphus is a decentralized clinical trials management platform built on the Ethereum blockchain. Essentially, it takes data from users and uploads it to the blockchain. Using external sources, such as a physician or insurance company, the data is verified and made immutable. Then, researchers can use the data to acquire participants for their studies, while using our fully customizable study builder to efficiently build studies in accordance to regulatory standards.

Who can use Delphus?

Delphus is currently live on the Ethereum test network. Anyone can give it a try and run their studies on it. We are open to beta testers, so if you are interested, please contact us.

Where are you based?

We are in the Boston area; however, we are also connected with several advisors that live in the Bay Area.

How are you raising funds?

Due to the unclear legal framework for ICOs in the United States and the negative attitude that the wider crypto community has toward them, we are not planning on holding an ICO. However, we are engaged with standard types of investment; if you are interested, please make any inquiries here.
